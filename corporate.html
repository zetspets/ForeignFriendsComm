Design Elements

Please provide a list of design elements alongside their justifications:

  • A colour scheme (with HTML colour codes, which must be consistent with your final website) — see the example below from the BUas brand book:
    • #078C41 The green stands for  
    • #f22233 Dark blue signifies…
    • #23c0d5 The orange colour refers to the Dutch identity and the city of Breda (Oranjestad). Orange stands for…
    • #f26e22 The orange colour refers to the Dutch identity and the city of Breda (Oranjestad). Orange stands for…
    • #813418 The orange colour refers to the Dutch identity and the city of Breda (Oranjestad). Orange stands for…
  • The initial idea for our logo was a variety of petals with all kinds of colors, from sharp to soft and from bright to dark, symbolizing diversity of people, mindsets and cultures, all united in one circle in the middle, as in some countries there are even traditional dances where people are holding hands creating a moving circle which symbolizes unity and friendship, which in the end results in a beautiful flower which symbolize the values of our company and how inclusivity and open-mindedness can create something wonderful. In the final version we needed to cut a bit our flower to include the name of the company to properly follow the rules of logo but the main ideas behind our design remain the same. 
  • As a font it was chosen Agrandir family fonts and the reason might be a little surprising: it is very basic! Let me explain: we wanted to appear as neutral as possible as we promote interculturalism and Agrandir is perfect for it, it is not too think, not too thin or not too casual so anyone can interpret it in their own way based on their background and mindset.
  • User interface patterns (e.g. grids, carousels, menu organizations etc.)
  • The most important information is plastered on the home page for ease to the user. As our target group consists of people aged 18-29, it is important that the grabbing factors of our brand and product are quick and easy to find upon first glance. More in-depth information about the brand and the team are on the 'About Us' page as this information is still important but since this content would take more words than visual aspects, it would take away from the crucial information on the home page. Of course the content that surrounds the products we offer is on the shop page to give the user easy access.        
  •  The home page features a carousel of images to quickly grab the attention of the viewer with buttons that will direct them around the website relating to the text on the image (EX: "Our Cultures" leads to the 'About Us' page). There is also a grid in the middle displaying the sample products as a way to cohesively show off our product. On the 'About Us' page there are cards displaying our main values as the card become the focal point causing the user to pay more attention to the values. The cards are also more pleasing to the eye as the lines create direction.

    • How does website design fit the marketing and communication strategy?
    • How does website design help showcase the unique value proposal of the product?

Management

Lean Canvas

Fill in at least 2 bullet points per building block and according to the rubrics.

Problem

  • List your top 3 problems you solve for your target group(s).
  • Describe briefly how the problems are solved now (existing alternatives).

Solution

  • Outline the brand solution(s) you have for the above problem(s).

Customer segments

  • List your target customers and/or users.
  • Describe briefly the characteristics of your ideal customer (early adopter, brand persona).

Unique value proposition

  • With a single, clear compelling message, state why your brand idea is different and worth paying attention to.

Unfair advantage

  • List the aspect(s) of your brand that cannot easily be copied, also called your sustainable competitive advantage(s).

Channels

  • List your main path to customers.
  • How do you reach them? What channel(s) do you use.

Key metrics

  • List the key numbers that tell you how your brand experience is doing.
  • For example amount of users, downloads, visitors, subscriptions, sales etc. Numbers you are able to measure.

Revenue streams

  • List your sources of revenue: describe the revenue model and the (different) revenue stream(s).

Cost structure

  • List your main costs: define the fixed and variable costs.
  • Calculate the cost per unit.

Services/products

  • State what your product and/or service is and how this contributes to your unique value proposition.
  • Clarify the fit between the product/service developed and the brand identity/brand image.
